What is Usher's net worth? Sapphire rep says Vegas &quot;fake dollar&quot; claims are BS!

A representative from the Las Vegas strip club has said that the “Usher throwing fake money” claim is false. But whether we’re counting Usherbucks or not, what is the singer’s real net worth?

Lately, Twitter users have been discussing news reports alleging that American singer Usher handed out fake money to a stripper. On April 12, 2021, reports surfaced claiming that Usher had handed out fake ‘Usher bucks’ to a stripper at the Sapphire club in Vegas. Recently a representative from the club has come forward to contest these claims.

What is Usher’s net worth?

According to the Celebrity net worth portal, the American singer/songwriter is worth $180 million. The 42-year-old singer has sold more than 75 million records globally so far. As a result, this makes him one of the best-selling artists of all time.

Aside from his impeccable music career, Usher has also invested in many businesses. He is also the co-founder of RBMG Records, which is Justin Bieber’s record company.

The singer’s career began when he was 13 and was signed by LaFace Records in 1991. However, things really took off when he released his first-ever album in 1994. Thanks to his first self titled album, he became one of the youngest artists of all times to find a spot in the Billboard charts. He was only 16 when his first album peaked to No.25 on the charts.

Over the course of 30 years of his career, several of the singer’s albums have been on the US Billboard 200 charts.

The singer also holds numerous awards like the Grammys and Billboard Awards. He also owns various properties across the United States.

According to the Celebrity Net worth report, one of his most recent purchases is his home in the Hollywood Hills, which he bought in 2015. The property cost him $3.37 million. He sold it off in 2018 for $3.3 million.

Sapphire Club Rep says ‘Usher fake bucks’ claims not true

No, Usher did not give ‘fake bucks’ to the women at the Sapphire Club.

In an interview with TMZ, a rep for the club cleared the air around the false claims. The rep revealed that Usher had not tipped the strippers with fake money.

The representative went on to clarify that the singer had dished out authentic money on the dancers and not fake ‘Usher dollars’. He had in fact spent thousands on the women at the club.

Moreover, sources close to Usher stated that it was somebody from Usher’s crew who had left the ‘Usher fake bucks’ behind. They did it both as a joke and also to promote his brand new Vegas residency.

Usher reacts to the fake money controversy

The singer took to his Instagram on April 12, to offer a response of sorts to the controversy. He posted the picture sitting on a chair with the fake Usher dollars cap on his head. The bucket hat features $1 dollar bills from Usher’s fake money.
